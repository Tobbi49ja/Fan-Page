About

Juice WRLD (real name: Jarad Anthony Higgins) was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter born on December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois. He rose to fame with his breakout hit "Lucid Dreams" in 2018, which sampled Sting's "Shape of My Heart" and peaked at No. 2 on the Billboard Hot 100.


Juice WRLD was known for his emo-rap style, blending hip-hop with themes of heartbreak, mental health, and drug use. His music often featured freestyles and emotionally raw lyrics that resonated with a younger generation. Other popular tracks include "All Girls Are the Same," "Robbery," "Legends," and "Wishing Well."


Tragically, he passed away on December 8, 2019, at just 21 years old, after suffering a seizure at Chicago's Midway Airport, reportedly due to an accidental drug overdose. Despite his short life, Juice WRLD left a major impact on the music world and is remembered as one of the leading voices of a new wave of genre-blending artists. His posthumous albums, including "Legends Never Die" (2020), have continued to find success.

career

Juice WRLD, born Jarad Higgins in 1998, was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter known for his emotionally raw lyrics and genre-blending style that fused hip-hop, rock, and emo influences. He rose to fame in 2018 with his breakout single “Lucid Dreams,” which became a multi-platinum hit and peaked at No. 2 on the Billboard Hot 100.




His debut album, Goodbye & Good Riddance (2018), showcased his talent for freestyle and emotionally driven music, while his follow-up project, Death Race for Love (2019), debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200. Juice WRLD quickly became a voice of a generation dealing with heartbreak, mental health struggles, and substance use.




Tragically, he passed away in December 2019 at the age of 21, just days after his birthday. Despite his short career, Juice WRLD left a lasting impact, with posthumous releases like Legends Never Die (2020) to keep his legacy alive.
